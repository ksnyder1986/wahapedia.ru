Gotrek vs the Mortal Realms

This weekend, the greatest monster hunter the world-that-was ever knew is coming to the Mortal Realms. Gotrek Gurnisson, fabled hero of books and audio dramas, is returning to tabletops after a long absence.* As you might expect, he’s an absolute beast. Don’t believe us? Check out his warscroll. We’ll give you a minute to let it sink in.



We wanted to see if he’s really as good as he seems (spoiler alert: he is), so we decided to pit him against a variety of adversaries. We recruited Niall from Warhammer Glasgow to help us roll the dice – thanks Niall!** – and got started.



Grey Seer Thanquol is – in his own mind, at least – the Slayer’s ultimate nemesis, so it only seems fitting that he had the first chance to kill Gotrek. We gave Thanquol priority in the first round, as he would certainly attack from the shadows when Gotrek least expects it. His first attacks were reasonably successful, with both Thanquol and his rat ogor bodyguard, Boneripper, causing one wound each to the Slayer.



Gotrek’s return attack caused six wounds, which seemed survivable – the skaven might have a chance after all! Then we remembered that Gotrek attacks again at the end of the combat phase, and even the Protection of the Horned Rat couldn’t save Thanquol from a total of eight wounds, ending a centuries-long grudge in a single combat round.

One of the Slayer’s greatest deeds back during the End Times was facing Greater Daemons of each of the four Chaos Gods simultaneously. There was only one way to find out if he could repeat the feat…





We rolled for priority and Gotrek won. The Keeper of Secrets tried to entice him with its Dark Temptations, but Gotrek stubbornly refused, and even more stubbornly ignored the mortal wounds he took in return. Suddenly the daemons started to look a bit nervous.

Gotrek targeted the Wrath of Khorne Bloodthirster first and, after rolling a ludicrous number of 6s to hit, caused a total of 12 wounds to the Greater Daemon of Khorne. It struck back, but thanks to some lucky rolls for the Shoulder Plate of Edessa, Gotrek took no damage. Between them, the other three daemons caused an embarrassingly low three wounds to the Slayer. Gotrek struck again at the end of the combat phase, and easily finished off the Bloodthirster. The Keeper of Secrets’ ritual knife caused him a mortal wound… which he negated.

The second round saw Gotrek again resisting the Dark Temptation. A large number of 6s and a plethora of mortal wounds later, the Keeper of Secrets joined the Bloodthirster in death. The Great Unclean One and Lord of Change each managed to cause a wound to Gotrek, bringing him to 3 remaining… and in return, he took his axe to the Tzeentch daemon’s neck and killed it with his end of combat attacks, causing 19 unsaved wounds.



With a third successful priority roll under his belt, Gotrek caused 12 wounds to the Great Unclean One – taking none in return from its Blubber and Bile. The ailing daemon failed to hurt him, and Gotrek’s second set of attacks finished it off in style, though this time he took 2 wounds in return.

Okay, he can kill a rat and some daemons, but what about a god? Well, turns out the answer is yes. Yes, he can kill a god.



Nagash attacked first, causing a mighty, er, one wound to Gotrek. The Slayer’s two sets of attacks caused 14 in return, even with Nagash ignoring wounds on a 4+. In the second round of combat, Nagash had priority again, and managed three wounds on the Slayer… who retaliated with the fury of Grimnir, making the Supreme Lord of the Undead seem a little less than supreme.

Time to pull out the real big gun. Archaon literally destroyed Gotrek’s world – surely he can finish the job and take out the Slayer too? Well, that would take better dice rolls than he had, really. The Slayer of Kings failed to slay, or even scratch, the Slayer, though his daemonic steed Dorghar did slightly better, causing 1 wound.



Gotrek’s riposte caused 11 wounds to Archaon, with the Armour of Morkar bouncing two back to the Slayer. His second set of attacks was even more effective, causing another 16 unsaved wounds (including one to himself), and proving the Everchosen to be more like the Neverchosen.

Gotrek is, as we’ve discovered, one of the best Monster and Hero killers in the Age of Sigmar. But what about hordes? Say, 40 Moonclan Grot Stabbas with pokin’ spears? They’ll get the job done, right? Not even the Slayer can carve his way through all of those before they wear him down.



We even let the grots attack first, and they enthusiastically poked away, causing three wounds on Gotrek – not bad at all! That would add up over a few rounds of combat, right? Alas, they couldn’t survive one, with Gotrek killing a frankly ludicrous 27 of them with his first batch of attacks, and polishing off the remaining 13 with the second. They didn’t even get to take a battleshock test.



So there you have it, folks. What can Gotrek kill? Anything you put in front of him, basically. Of course, battlefield conditions are different – with arrows and spells flying, there might be more of a chance… maybe. So when you get your Gotrek on the table,*** let us know on the Warhammer Age of Sigmar Facebook page how he gets on!

You can pre-order Gotrek, alongside his new novel Ghoulslayer and audio drama Realmslayer: Blood of the Old World, this Saturday. Sign up to the Warhammer Community and Black Library newsletters now, and we’ll let you know when it’s all available to buy.

* Gotrek and his companion Felix had models and rules back in Warhammer Fantasy Battles, but that was a very long time ago indeed.
** Thanks to Stuart as well, for a fabulous cup of tea. Staying hydrated is very important during gruelling combat.
*** He can be included as an ally in ANY Order army, even if his whopping 520 points cost exceeds the usual allies allowance – and we’re sure we’ll be seeing him pop up all over the place.
